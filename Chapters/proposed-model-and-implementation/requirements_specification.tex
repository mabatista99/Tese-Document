\section{Requirements}
\label{sec:RequirementsSpecification}

The use cases define what the platform is expected to support from the user's point of view. The following tables translate those goals into functional requirements, which describe the required system capabilities, and non-functional requirements, which define the main quality constraints that shape the design.

\subsection{Functional Requirements}

Table~\ref{tab:functional-requirements} presents the functional requirements
specified for the platform. In particular, they cover user authentication,
device registration and provisioning, telemetry ingestion, anomaly detection,
real-time monitoring, historical queries, notifications, and operation at the
edge during temporary connectivity loss.

\begin{table}[H]
    \centering
    \caption{Functional requirements.}
    \label{tab:functional-requirements}
    \small
    \rowcolors{2}{gray!8}{}
    \begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{l X l}
        \toprule
        \textbf{ID} & \textbf{Description}                                                                                                                        & \textbf{UC(s)} \\
        \midrule
        FR01        & The system must allow users to register and authenticate before accessing protected platform functions.                                     & All            \\
        FR02        & The system must allow users to register devices and provision them for cloud or edge deployment.                                            & UC01, UC05     \\
        FR03        & The system must ingest telemetry from \gls{IoT} devices over multiple communication protocols.                                              & UC01           \\
        FR04        & The system must allow devices to upload binary files for later processing by the platform.                                                  & UC01           \\
        FR05        & The system must identify and register newly observed telemetry fields without previous manual schema configuration.                         & UC01           \\
        FR06        & The system must allow users to create, edit, enable, disable, and delete monitoring rules with configurable conditions and severity levels. & UC02           \\
        FR07        & The system must detect abnormal conditions in telemetry and generate alerts.                                                                & UC02           \\
        FR08        & The system must provide authenticated users with real-time telemetry and alert updates for the devices they monitor.                        & UC01, UC03     \\
        FR09        & The system must allow users to review, filter, acknowledge, and retrieve past alerts.                                                       & UC03, UC04     \\
        FR10        & The system must allow users to query historical telemetry for a device over a selected time range.                                          & UC04           \\
        FR11        & The system must notify users when abnormal conditions are detected, according to their configured notification preferences.                 & UC02           \\
        FR12        & The edge node must record telemetry and alerts locally while cloud connectivity is unavailable.                                             & UC05           \\
        FR13        & The edge node must synchronize locally buffered telemetry and alerts with the cloud after connectivity is restored.                         & UC05           \\
        FR14        & The edge node must obtain and keep updated the monitoring rules required for local evaluation.                                              & UC05           \\
        \bottomrule
    \end{tabularx}
\end{table}

\subsection{Non-Functional Requirements}

Table~\ref{tab:non-functional-requirements} presents the specified
non-functional requirements. In particular, they define the main quality goals
related to latency, heterogeneous payload support, extensibility, offline
resilience, scalability, security, and data preservation.

\begin{table}[H]
    \centering
    \caption{Non-functional requirements.}
    \label{tab:non-functional-requirements}
    \small
    \rowcolors{2}{gray!8}{}
    \begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{l X l}
        \toprule
        \textbf{ID} & \textbf{Description}                                                                                                                & \textbf{UC(s)}   \\
        \midrule
        NFR01       & The platform must keep telemetry ingestion latency low enough for live monitoring under supported load.                             & UC01             \\
        NFR02       & The platform must accept telemetry payloads with different field structures without requiring device-specific schema configuration. & UC01             \\
        NFR03       & The platform must allow new processing capabilities to be added without changing the existing ingestion flow.                       & All              \\
        NFR04       & The edge deployment must continue monitoring during temporary loss of cloud connectivity.                                           & UC05             \\
        NFR05       & The platform must allow backend components to scale independently according to its needs.                                           & All              \\
        NFR06       & The platform must restrict access to authorized users and devices, and it must ensure secure communication with external entities.  & All              \\
        NFR07       & The platform must preserve ingested telemetry and alerts data during temporary failures until it is stored or synchronized.         & UC01, UC03, UC05 \\
        \bottomrule
    \end{tabularx}
\end{table}

The following sections describe the platform architecture and explain how it addresses these requirements.
